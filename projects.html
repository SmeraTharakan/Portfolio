Self Stabilizing Spoon for Parikson's Disease


Parkinsonâ€™s disease, the second most common neurodegenerative condition, predominantly affects older individuals, causing significant nerve cell damage that hampers movement and daily functioning. In response, our team of three developed a spoon utilizing Arduino Nano and MPU6050 Gyroaccelerometer, aiming to significantly enhance the lives of those with Parkinson's.Our innovation comprises a self-stabilizing mechanism integrated into the spoon's handle. This mechanism compensates for involuntary tremors or shivers experienced by users, ensuring the spoon's bowl remains stable. The Gyro sensor detects hand position, while the Arduino Nano controller calculates the error between the current and zero positions. Determining the axis of tremor, the Nano board controls two SG90 servo motors, each correcting tremors along a separate axis by adjusting the spoon's angle. Consequently, the spoon's head remains steady, independent of the handle's movement. This device represents a beacon of hope for Parkinson's sufferers, offering a lightweight, compact, user-friendly, and adaptable solution.
